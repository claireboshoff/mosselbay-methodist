About Our Church

A community of faith rooted in love, service, and the Wesleyan tradition since the early 1900s.

Our Minister

Rev. Lynne Walters

Rev. Lynne Walters has been leading the Mossel Bay Methodist congregation since December 2014. With a deep commitment to pastoral care and community transformation, she brings warmth, wisdom, and a genuine love for people to every service and interaction.

Lynne entered full-time ministry in 1997 and was ordained in August 2000. Her ministry is characterised by a heart for healing — reflected in the church's ministries of GriefShare, counselling, and community support.

"Our calling is to proclaim the Gospel of Jesus Christ for the healing and transformation of all people."

— Rev. Lynne Walters

Our Heritage

Over a Century of Faith

Mossel Bay Methodist Church has been a pillar of the community for over 120 years.

Early 1900s

Methodist worship established in Mossel Bay as part of the growing Wesleyan mission in the Southern Cape.

1903

Junior Church ministry begins — a continuous ministry to children that has run for over 120 years.

1982

The MCSA celebrates its centenary. Ben Matthee designs the iconic cross and shell logo still used today.

2014

Rev. Lynne Walters appointed as minister. A new season of healing-focused ministry begins.

Today

An active, caring congregation with GriefShare, counselling, Women's Ministry, and Junior Church.

Our Denomination

The Methodist Church of Southern Africa

We are part of the Methodist Church of Southern Africa (MCSA), the largest mainline Protestant denomination in Southern Africa. The MCSA serves over 5,000 congregations across South Africa, Namibia, Botswana, Lesotho, Eswatini, and Mozambique.

Rooted in the Wesleyan tradition, we emphasise:

  • Personal holiness — Growing in faith and character
  • Social justice — Standing with the marginalised and vulnerable
  • Community transformation — Making a difference where we live

Our Mission

What We Believe

Healing

We believe God heals broken hearts, broken relationships, and broken communities. Through prayer, care, and practical support, we are instruments of that healing.

Community

We are family. From Sunday worship to mid-week groups, from children to seniors — every person matters and every voice is heard.

Transformation

We proclaim the Gospel not just in words but in action — transforming lives, families, and our broader community through the love of Christ.
